POPX Documentation

A comprehensive family of TouchDesigner operators for advanced particle and instance manipulation.

Overview

POPX is a powerful ecosystem of TouchDesigner components designed to bring industry-standard particle and instance workflows to real-time GPU-accelerated environments. Built for artists, technical directors, and creative coders, POPX combines the flexibility of Houdini-style operators with TouchDesigner's performance-oriented architecture.

Whether you're creating procedural animations, real-time simulations, or data-driven installations, POPX provides the tools to manipulate thousands of particles and instances with precision and control.

Operator Families

Generators

5 Operators

Create and emit particles from various sources including geometry, surfaces, volumes, and procedural patterns.

Modifiers

8 Operators

Transform, deform, and manipulate particle streams with forces, noise, constraints, and custom expressions.

Falloffs

4 Operators

Create spatial influence fields that drive modifier strength based on distance, geometry, and custom curves.

Tools

6 Operators

Utilities for particle analysis, attribute management, instancing, and data conversion between formats.

Simulations

3 Operators

Physics-based solvers including FLIP fluids, SPH particles, and constraint-based dynamics systems.

Key Features

  • GPU-Accelerated Performance – All operators leverage TouchDesigner's compute shaders for real-time performance with thousands of particles.
  • Modular Architecture – Chain operators together to create complex procedural systems with full control over each stage.
  • Houdini-Style Workflow – Familiar parameter organization and attribute-based manipulation for VFX artists.
  • Custom Attributes – Create, modify, and delete custom particle attributes including vectors, quaternions, and matrices.
  • Instancing Support – Full support for instanced rendering with orientation, scale, and custom material attributes.
  • Simulation Systems – Industry-standard FLIP and SPH solvers with collision detection and constraints.
