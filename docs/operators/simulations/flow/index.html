Flow

Summary

Flow is a GPU-accelerated 3D fluid solver that simulates gaseous fluids using a 3D texture-based approach. This operator creates smoke, fire, and atmospheric effects by solving the Navier-Stokes equations for incompressible flow on the GPU. The simulation operates in a volumetric grid where velocity and substance density are stored as 3D textures, allowing for real-time interactive fluid dynamics.

The solver supports substance injection from POPX point sources, volumetric source inputs, and various forces including buoyancy, gravity, external force fields, and optical flow. You can control fluid behavior through viscosity (thickness), vorticity (swirling motion), pressure iterations (incompressibility accuracy), and dissipation rates for both velocity and substance density. The simulation maintains mass conservation through a pressure projection step and supports collision boundaries and obstacles defined via 3D textures.

Flow optionally generates particle systems that are advected by the fluid velocity field, creating wispy smoke trails or fire ember effects. Particles can spawn from density regions and inherit color from the substance field with customizable lifespans and variance. The simulation outputs velocity and substance density as 3D textures suitable for volume rendering or further processing, making it ideal for smoke plumes, fire effects, atmospheric disturbances, and abstract fluid art.

Page: Flow

General Settings Generalsettings – Header for simulation control parameters.
Reset Reset – Pulse to clear simulation and reset to initial state.
Time Step Timestep – Simulation speed multiplier per frame (higher = faster evolution).
Simulation Bounds Simulationbounds – Physical size of simulation volume in world units (XYZ).
Max Axis Resolution Maxaxisresolution – Maximum voxel resolution for longest axis (other axes scale proportionally).
Simulation Resolution Simulationresolution – Read-only display of actual grid resolution (XYZ voxels).
Precision Precision – Texture precision for simulation data (16-bit or 32-bit float).
Velocity Velocity – Header for velocity field parameters.
Velocity Dissipation Velocitydissipation – Rate at which velocity decays over time (0 = no damping, 1 = instant decay).
Pressure Iterations Pressureiterations – Number of iterations for pressure solve (higher = more accurate incompressibility).
Viscosity Viscosity – Fluid thickness controlling resistance to flow (0 = inviscid, higher = thicker).
Diffusion Iterations Diffusioniterations – Solver iterations for viscosity diffusion (only active when viscosity > 0).
Vorticity Vorticity – Vorticity confinement strength to restore rotational motion (creates swirling detail).
Substance Substance – Header for substance density parameters.
Substance Dissipation Substancedissipation – Rate at which substance density fades over time (higher = faster fade).
Substance Color Dissipation Substancecolordissipation – Per-channel dissipation rates for RGB color (allows colored smoke to fade unevenly).

Inputs

Input 0 – Injection Points (Optional) – POPX points for substance and force injection into simulation
Input 1 – Source Density (Optional) – 3D texture for continuous substance density addition
Input 2 – Source Color (Optional) – 3D texture for continuous color addition
Input 3 – Source Force (Optional) – 3D texture with RGB velocity vectors for force addition
Input 4 – Source Temperature (Optional) – 3D texture for continuous temperature addition

Outputs

Output 0 – Velocity – 3D texture containing RGB velocity field vectors
Output 1 – Substance – 3D texture with RGBA substance density and color
Output 2 – Particles (Optional) – POP particles advected by velocity field (when Advect enabled)
