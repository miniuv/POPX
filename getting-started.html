Getting Started with POPX

POPX Overview

POPX is a new family of operators that extends TouchDesigner's POPs (Point Operators). It introduces a GPU-accelerated framework for generating, transforming, and simulating geometry in real time. While optimized for its packed POPX Geometry system, all operators work seamlessly with any standard POP geometry.

POPX Geometry

A packed structure where each piece of geometry is represented by a single point containing all transform attributes — position, rotation, scale, and pivot. This compact format allows transformations to operate directly on the GPU without manually constructing attributes or computing transformation matrices — it handles everything for you.

Generators

Generate instances for the POPX system, either by creating new geometry, converting existing geometry to POPX instances, or producing general geometry structures.

Falloffs

Define spatial regions of influence, using the popxFalloff attribute to control how strongly other operators affect instances.

Modifiers

Adjust and transform geometry by modifying position, rotation, scale, pivot, and other custom attributes such as color.

Tools

Provide additional utilities for visualization, orientation, geometry management, and general workflow assistance.

Simulations

A stand-alone part of the POPX ecosystem focused on time-based physical simulations such as fluid, particle, and growth systems.
